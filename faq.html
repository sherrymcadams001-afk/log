Most forested properties with at least 20 acres of mature timber are suitable for some form of harvest. Key factors include tree species, age (typically 40+ years for softwoods, 60+ for hardwoods), road access, and terrain. We offer free initial consultations to assess your property's potential. Our LiDAR surveys provide precise valuations once you decide to move forward.

We offer several contract types: lump-sum (fixed price for marked timber), pay-as-cut (payment based on actual volume harvested), and share arrangements. Most contracts include provisions for access, liability, site cleanup, and payment schedules. All contracts are reviewed by our legal team and can be customized to your preferences. Typical terms range from 6 months to 2 years depending on the harvest size.

Timeline varies by project size and complexity. A typical 100-acre selective harvest: 2-4 weeks for planning and permitting, 4-8 weeks for harvesting operations, and 2-4 weeks for site restoration. We provide detailed timelines during the planning phase and keep you informed throughout the process via our client portal.

Our "Stewards First" philosophy means we leave your property better than we found it. Post-harvest activities include: water bar installation on roads, spreading slash on skid trails, seeding disturbed areas, and our 3-for-1 reforestation program (we plant three seedlings for every tree harvested). Most properties show significant regrowth within 2-3 years.

Payment is based on species, grade, and volume of timber harvested, multiplied by current market prices. Our LiDAR inventory provides accurate pre-harvest estimates. Final payment is calculated using certified scale tickets from the receiving mill. We provide transparent accounting and typically pay within 30 days of delivery.

üõ∞Ô∏è About Technology

LiDAR (Light Detection and Ranging) uses laser pulses to create incredibly detailed 3D maps. Unlike traditional ground-based cruising, LiDAR captures data on every tree, provides sub-meter accuracy, and can cover 200+ acres per day. It also penetrates forest canopy to map terrain. This results in 15-25% more accurate volume estimates compared to traditional sampling methods.

Our LiDAR-based inventories typically achieve ¬±5% accuracy for total volume estimates at the stand level, compared to ¬±15-20% for traditional cruise methods. Individual tree detection rates exceed 95% for trees over 6" DBH. All estimates are validated against ground-truth plots and historical harvest data.

No, you don't need to be present. We just need permission to access your property on the scheduled survey dates. Our pilots operate autonomously following pre-approved flight plans. We'll coordinate access details and notify you of results. You can track survey progress through our client portal.

We deliver data in industry-standard formats: LAS/LAZ (point clouds), GeoTIFF (raster DEMs and canopy height models), Shapefile/GeoPackage (vector layers), and KMZ (Google Earth). All datasets include complete metadata and coordinate system documentation. Custom formats available upon request.

üå± Carbon Credits

Basic requirements: 40+ acres of contiguous forestland, clear title to the property, forest cover for at least 10 years (no recent deforestation), and willingness to commit to a 20+ year management agreement. Some programs have additional requirements based on location or forest type. We offer a free eligibility assessment.

Earnings vary based on forest type, location, and market conditions. Typical ranges: $15-40 per ton of CO2 sequestered. A well-managed 100-acre forest might sequester 250-400 tons annually, generating $3,750-16,000/year. Our calculator provides personalized estimates based on your specific property characteristics.

Yes, in most programs. "Improved Forest Management" (IFM) projects allow sustainable harvesting as long as overall carbon stocks increase over time. Some programs restrict the intensity or timing of harvests. We help you design management plans that optimize both timber revenue and carbon credit income.

Initial project development takes 6-12 months (baseline inventory, project design, third-party verification). Once verified, credits are issued annually. Payment timing depends on your sales agreement‚Äîsome buyers pay upfront, others upon credit issuance. We offer advance payment options against future credits for qualified projects.

Carbon credit agreements typically transfer with the land. New owners inherit both the benefits (ongoing credit revenue) and obligations (management commitments). We facilitate smooth transitions and can provide documentation for real estate transactions. Some programs allow buyout provisions under specific circumstances.

üåç Sustainability

We maintain FSC Chain of Custody (FSC-C123456), PEFC endorsement, SFI Certified Sourcing, and are certified to APA PRG 320 for CLT products. All certifications are audited annually by accredited third-party bodies. Certification documents are available upon request.

Our practices include: selective harvesting (no clearcutting), streamside management zones (50-150ft buffers), seasonal timing to protect soil, designated skid trails with slash mats, wildlife habitat retention (snags, den trees), and our 3-for-1 reforestation program. We exceed all state and federal environmental regulations.

For every tree we harvest, we plant three climate-adapted seedlings. Since 2010, we've planted over 2 million trees. We use locally-sourced genetic stock matched to site conditions, with 5-year survival monitoring showing 85%+ establishment rates. Reforestation is included at no additional cost to landowners.
